CASE: Air Products and Chemicals, Inc. v. Airgas, Inc.
COURT: Delaware Supreme Court
YEAR: 2011
DOCTRINE: Defensive measures / poison pill / Unocal enhanced scrutiny
KEY TOPIC: Board authority to maintain poison pill against hostile bidder

SUMMARY:
This case addresses whether a board of directors may maintain a poison pill to block a hostile tender offer that the board believes undervalues the company. The Delaware Supreme Court affirmed that directors may refuse to redeem a poison pill if they reasonably conclude that the bid poses a threat to corporate policy and effectiveness under the Unocal standard.

KEY LEGAL PRINCIPLES:

1. Unocal enhanced scrutiny
When directors adopt defensive measures in response to a takeover threat, they must show:
- reasonable grounds for believing a threat exists, and
- that the defensive response is reasonable in relation to that threat.

2. Board authority to reject inadequate offers
Directors may determine in good faith that a hostile tender offer is inadequate and may continue to maintain defensive measures such as a poison pill.

3. Substantive coercion
A board may treat the risk that shareholders will accept an inadequate offer as a legitimate threat to corporate policy and effectiveness.

IMPORTANT HOLDING:
The court held that Airgas's board acted within its fiduciary duties by refusing to redeem the poison pill and rejecting Air Products’ hostile tender offer.
