Controller Transactions establishes the governing framework for one fiduciary setting, whereas Stockholder Vote Cleansing governs a distinct one.

Rule:  
The standard of review for controller transactions requires entire fairness unless the transaction is conditioned from the outset on approval by both an independent special committee and an informed, uncoerced majority of the minority, thereby invoking business judgment review. In contrast, where no controller conflict exists, a fully informed and uncoerced vote of disinterested stockholders cleanses the transaction, shifting review to the business judgment rule. Thus, the duty of loyalty and good faith are satisfied when stockholders, acting with full information and without coercion, ratify the board’s decision, obviating the need for heightened scrutiny.
