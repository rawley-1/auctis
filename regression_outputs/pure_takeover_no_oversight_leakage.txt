Marchand clarifies, in line with Stone's rule that failure to act in good faith is a breach of the duty of loyalty, that directors must make a good faith effort to implement an oversight system and monitor it. Taken together, Caremark defines liability at the point of an utter failure to attempt to assure a reasonable reporting or information system exists, whereas Marchand requires good-faith monitoring once an oversight system is implemented.

Rule:  
Directors breach the duty of loyalty where their conduct constitutes a failure to act in good faith.

Analysis:  
This matters because Delaware doctrine distinguishes baseline fiduciary obligations from conduct that crosses into breach. The significance is that directors act inconsistently with the duty of loyalty where their conduct constitutes a failure to act in good faith. As a result, breach arises only when inaction or conscious disregard moves beyond imperfection and into disloyal conduct.
