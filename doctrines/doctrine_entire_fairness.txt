RULE:
Entire fairness is Delaware’s most rigorous standard of review and requires fiduciaries to establish both fair dealing and fair price when they stand on both sides of a transaction or are otherwise conflicted.


KEY_DISTINCTION:
Entire fairness examines both the process and the price of a transaction, whereas business judgment review defers to disinterested and independent decision-making.


ANCHOR_PHRASES:
- entire fairness
- fair dealing
- fair price
- stands on both sides
- controller transaction
- burden of proof


APPLICATION:
Use when fiduciaries are conflicted, a controller is involved, or independence is compromised, unless procedural protections such as MFW apply.
