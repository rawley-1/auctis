SECTION: Governing legal standard


CASE: Revlon, Inc. v. MacAndrews & Forbes Holdings, Inc.
COURT: Delaware Supreme Court
YEAR: 1986
DOCTRINE: Revlon duties / sale of control
AUTHORITY: Binding precedent
KEY TOPIC: Maximization of shareholder value, auction duty


The duty of the board changes from preservation of the corporate entity to maximization of the company's value at a sale for the stockholders' benefit. Directors become auctioneers charged with getting the best price reasonably available when sale or breakup of the company becomes inevitable.
